Fundraising Event Ideas

Organize fundraising events such as bake sales, car washes, charity auctions, or benefit concerts. Leverage the support of volunteers, sponsors, and local venues to host these events and generate revenue.

Small Fundraising Events:

  • Bake Sale: Host a bake sale featuring homemade treats such as cookies, brownies, cupcakes, and pies. Set up a booth at a local park, community center, or outside a popular store to attract passersby.
  • Car Wash: Organize a car wash where participants pay a fee to have their cars washed by volunteers. Choose a visible location with access to water and gather supplies such as soap, sponges, and hoses.
  • Charity Garage Sale: Collect donations of gently used items from community members and hold a charity garage sale. Set up tables to display items such as clothing, toys, books, and household goods, and price them affordably.
  • Pancake Breakfast: Host a pancake breakfast event where attendees purchase tickets to enjoy a delicious meal of pancakes, sausages, and beverages. Consider partnering with a local restaurant or securing donations of food items.
  • Trivia Night: Organize a trivia night at a local venue such as a restaurant, bar, or community center. Charge an entry fee for teams to participate and offer prizes for the top-scoring teams. You can also incorporate raffles or silent auctions for additional fundraising.
  • Fitness Challenge: Plan a fitness challenge such as a fun run, walkathon, or yoga session. Participants can register and collect pledges from friends and family based on the distance covered or the duration of the activity.
  • DIY Craft Fair: Host a DIY craft fair where artisans and crafters can showcase and sell their handmade goods. Charge a small vendor fee for table space and encourage attendees to support local artists by purchasing unique gifts and crafts.
  • Movie Night Fundraiser: Organize a movie night fundraiser at a local venue or outdoor space. Charge admission for entry and screen popular films or family-friendly movies. Offer concessions such as popcorn, candy, and drinks for additional revenue.
  • Pet Parade or Petting Zoo: Host a pet-themed event such as a pet parade, petting zoo, or pet costume contest. Participants can register their pets and pay a fee to participate, while attendees can enjoy interacting with animals and supporting the cause.
  • Cook-Off Competition: Organize a cook-off competition such as a chili cook-off, BBQ cook-off, or dessert competition. Participants can enter their culinary creations for judging, and attendees can purchase tasting tickets to sample the dishes and vote for their favorites.
